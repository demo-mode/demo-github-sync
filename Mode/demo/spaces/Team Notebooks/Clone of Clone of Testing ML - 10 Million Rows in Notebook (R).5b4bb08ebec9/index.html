CUSTOMER INSIGHTS REPORT


The objective of this report is:

  • to uncover hidden trends in customer purchases (Exploratory Data Analysis)
  • predict which previously purchased products will be in a customerâ€™s next order (Predictive Analysis)


The dataset used for this report contains a random sample of over 3 million orders from more than 200,000 customers. For each user, 4 to 100 of their prior orders are given, with the sequence of products purchased in each order. All orders made were between January to March 2019

EXPLORATORY DATA ANALYSIS


Seems like Sunday evenings and Monday mornings are the busiest, with almost 95% of the orders being placed between 8 AM and 9 PM

Produce is the most popular department followed by dairy eggs. In terms of aisles, fresh vegetables and fresh fruits are the most popular aisles

PURCHASE BEHAVIOR


  • The frequency plot of products in an order is a right-tailed distribution (with maximum value at 5)
  • Looks like customers order once in every week (check the peak at 7 days) or once in a month (peak at 30 days). We could also see smaller peaks at 14, 21 and 28 days (weekly intervals)

PREDICTIVE ANALYSIS


We build machine learning models that predict what product will the customer purchase in the next basket. This is a classification problem, as well as a regression of probability of repurchases.


As a baseline, we build a Naive Prediction model that predict the basket based on just the last order of a user.

This model has an Accuracy (F Score) of 21.5%, which is not very good.
